  1. Elastic electron-proton scattering (Relatvistic Quantum Mechanics Course, 2023)
    2. I made this project in my fifth semester as an assignment for my relativistic quantum mechanics course. I calculated the differential cross section for elastic electron-proton scattering, known as Rosenbluth's formula. I start with drawing the Feynman diagram for the scattering. From there, I analyzed the diagram using Dirac equation to find the invariant amplitude. After I obtained the amplitude, I calculated the differential cross section and obtained the infamous Rosenbluth's formula : $$\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}=\frac{\alpha^2}{4E^2\sin^4{\left(\dfrac{\theta}{2}\right)}\frac{E'}{E} \left[\left(\frac{G_E^2+\tau G_M^2}{1+\tau}\right)\cos^2{\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}+2\tau G_M^2\sin^2{\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}\right]$$
