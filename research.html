What is a kaon? Why should we care about it?

My undergraduate research is on kaon production. What is a kaon? Kaons (K mesons) are a group of four mesons distinguished by a unique strangeness quantum number. The four kaons are \(K^+\) (\(u\bar{s}\)), \(K^-\) (\(s\bar{u}\)) , \(K^0\) (\(d\bar{s}\)), and \(\bar{K}^0\) (\(s\bar{d}\)). Kaons can be produced by bombarding nucleons with photons. If we use real photons, the reaction is called photoproduction. If we use virtual photons, however, the reaction is then called electroproduction. Electroproduction is clearly the general form of photoproduction as the photons don't have to be on-shell. The reaction for the electroproduction of kaons off nucleons is given by

$$e(k_i)+p(p)\to e(k_f)+K^+(q)+\Lambda(p_\Lambda) .$$

The electroproduction reaction above can be regarded as a virtual photoproduction:

$$\gamma_\text{v}(k)+p(p)\to K^+(q)+\Lambda(p_\Lambda) .$$

In general, we write the reaction as

$$\gamma_\text{v}(k)+N(p)\to K(q)+Y(p_Y) ,$$

where $N$ is a nucleon, $K$ is a kaon, and $Y$ is a hyperon. Why should we be interested? Kaons can provide us with insight in strangeness physics.
